# Ethical Statement

Chapter 5 of Ethical Machines showed me how important it is to really look at how technology affects society and others. An idea that stood out to me is that 'machines are not inherently ethical or unethical, rather it’s the people who design and use them who determine how they work.' This means that developers must consider the consequences of what they build, and ensure they aren't designing things for malicious purposes.

I think ethical technology makes sure fairness, transparency, and accountability and at the forefront of their development. Tech that effects people’s lives should be made in a way that minimizes harm and bias towards people. It’s not enough for something to just work, it also needs to be responsible and fair!

I want to be more thoughtful about how my work could affect others! Even small choices can have large implications, and I think it’s important to approach all decisions with intention and awareness of what they may or may not cause.
